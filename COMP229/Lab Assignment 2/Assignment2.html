The Samsung Galaxy S III is a multi-touch, slate-format smartphone designed, developed and marketed by Samsung Electronics that runs the Android operationg system. It has additional software features, expanded hardware, and a redesigned physique from its predeccesor, the Samsung Galaxy S II.

 

Depending on the country, the 4.8-inch (120 mm) smartphone comes with different processors and RAM capacity, and 4G LTE support. This device was launched with Android 4.0.4 "Ice Cream Sandwich" and was upgraded to Android 4.3 "Jelly Bean", and can be updated to Android 4.4 "Kitkat" on variants with 2 GB of RAM. The phone successor, the Samsung Galaxy S4, was announced on 14 March 2013 and was released the following month.
